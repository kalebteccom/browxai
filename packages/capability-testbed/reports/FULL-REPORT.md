## Executive summary

Overall pass rate was 181/198 = 91.4% of all manifest rows, or 181/196 = 92.3% if the two service-worker skips are excluded. The headline is good breadth after the RFC 0004 architecture-hardening work: the harness exercised the full declared 198-row MCP surface from `manifest.ts` lines 1-6, and broad read/navigation/action/storage/session/control behavior held up. The release signal is not "all green", but it is also not "15 product defects": the 15 requested non-pass tools triage to 9 browxai defect candidates, 4 harness assertion bugs, and 2 expected provider-behavior rows.

Release-readiness: this looks close enough for a targeted fix-and-rerun lane, not a broad architecture rollback. RFC 0004's thesis is that maintainability must be mechanized by tests and fitness functions, not asserted in prose (`docs/rfcs/0004-architecture-hardening.md` lines 10-13 and 95-103). This harness is acting as that mechanism: it found real behavior candidates in extraction/search, coordinate/touch gestures, cookie deletion, live locale, virtual clock, and file drop, while also exposing harness drift around `plan`, `page_archive`, `network_body`, and credentials-provider expectations. Ship readiness should require fixing or consciously accepting the 9 defect candidates, then rerunning this report; the harness bugs should be fixed before using this suite as a release gate.

Important taxonomy note: `manifest.ts` says "16 real capabilities + control" in `types.ts` lines 21-41, while `HARNESS_CAPABILITIES` in `manifest.ts` lines 306-326 lists 17 real names, including `credentials`. The report's `byCapability` block contains 14 real capability buckets plus `control` and has no rows for `byob-attach`, `clipboard`, or `stealth`; those are reported below as zero-row coverage gaps rather than silently omitted.
